The square root \(\sqrt{x}\) of a positive real number \(x\) is a positive real number \(y\) such that
\begin{equation*} y^2=x. \end{equation*}
Higher roots are defined analogously: The \(n\)-th root \(\sqrt[n]{x}\) of a postive real number \(x\) is a positive real number \(y\) such that
\begin{equation*} y^n=x. \end{equation*}
