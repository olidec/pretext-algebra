A light year (the distance that light travels in one year) is 9 460 730 472 581 kilometres, and the mass of a single water molecule is 0.000 000 000 000 000 000 000 0056 grams. Without scientific notation these numbers seem meaningless. We have difficulty processing very large or very small numbers. However, if we were to write the numbers as \(9.46\cdot 10^{12}\) km or \(5.6\cdot 10^{-24}\) g, they immediately become more clear.
